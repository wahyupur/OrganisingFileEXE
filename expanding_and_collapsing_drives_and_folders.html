Expanding and Collapsing Drives and Folders

If a disk or folder contains further folders (subfolders), it has a small white arrow displayed next to it in the folders list. By clicking the white arrow, you expand the object to show its subfolders. This is often referred to as a hierarchical folder structure as there is a top level folder, followed by another folder nested within it followed by either other folders or files.

 

When you expand the disk or folder, the white arrow changes to a black arrow and if you click the black arrow, the object collapses and its subfolders are hidden again.

Note: The way your folders list appears to begin with depends on how you start Windows Explorer.
